# Article 80 - Subdivision Design and Improvements

## 80.1 General

This Article establishes standards for the design and construction of subdivisions to ensure adequate infrastructure, public safety, and compatibility with the county's development goals.

## 80.2 Layout and Design Generally

Subdivisions shall be designed to:
1. Provide safe and efficient circulation for vehicles, pedestrians, and emergency services
2. Minimize environmental impacts and preserve natural features
3. Ensure adequate provision of utilities and public services
4. Create attractive and functional neighborhoods
5. Comply with all applicable county, state, and federal requirements

## 80.3 Required Infrastructure and Improvements

All subdivisions shall provide the following infrastructure and improvements as applicable:
- Streets and roadways in accordance with county standards
- Water supply systems meeting county and state requirements  
- Wastewater treatment systems as required
- Stormwater management facilities
- Utility easements and connections
- Street signs and traffic control devices

## 80.4 Lots

### A. Lot Standards
All lots shall:
1. Have adequate area and dimensions for their intended use
2. Meet minimum standards for the applicable zoning district
3. Have appropriate access to public streets
4. Be designed to accommodate required utilities

### B. Lot Lines and Orientations
Lot lines shall be designed to:
1. Minimize grading and site disturbance
2. Preserve natural drainage patterns where feasible
3. Provide reasonable building sites
4. Facilitate utility installation and maintenance

## 80.5 Blocks

Block design shall consider:
1. Appropriate block lengths for the development context
2. Pedestrian circulation and connectivity
3. Emergency vehicle access
4. Integration with surrounding development patterns

## 80.6 Pedestrian Circulation (Sidewalks and Trails)

Pedestrian facilities shall be provided as determined appropriate by the Planning and Zoning Commission and may include:
- Sidewalks along streets where needed for public safety
- Trail connections to parks and community facilities
- Pedestrian crossings at appropriate locations

## 80.7 Vehicular Circulation (Streets and Bridges)

### A. Street Design Standards
Streets shall be designed and constructed to county standards and shall:
1. Provide safe and efficient traffic circulation
2. Accommodate emergency vehicle access
3. Include appropriate drainage facilities
4. Meet county construction specifications

### B. Street Classification
Streets shall be classified according to their function:
- **Local streets** - Provide access to individual lots
- **Collector streets** - Collect traffic from local streets
- **Arterial streets** - Carry traffic between major destinations

## 80.8 Construction Standards for Vehicular Circulation (Streets and Bridges)

All street construction shall:
1. Meet county engineering standards and specifications
2. Include proper base preparation and paving materials
3. Provide adequate drainage infrastructure
4. Include required signage and traffic control devices
5. Be inspected and approved by county officials before acceptance

## 80.9 Water Supply and Fire Protection

### A. Water Supply Requirements
All subdivisions shall provide adequate water supply through:
1. Connection to public water systems where available
2. Private wells meeting county and state standards where public water is not available
3. Adequate pressure and flow for domestic and fire protection needs

### B. Fire Protection
Fire protection facilities shall include:
1. Adequate water pressure and flow for fire suppression
2. Fire hydrants at appropriate locations where public water is available
3. Emergency vehicle access to all lots
4. Compliance with fire department requirements

## 80.10 Wastewater Systems

Wastewater treatment shall be provided through:
1. Connection to public sewer systems where available
2. Individual on-site systems meeting county and state requirements
3. Community systems approved by appropriate regulatory agencies
4. Compliance with [Missouri Department of Natural Resources](https://dnr.mo.gov/) regulations

## 80.11 Other Utilities

Provisions shall be made for:
1. Electric power service
2. Telecommunications services  
3. Natural gas service where available
4. Cable television/internet services
5. Adequate easements for utility installation and maintenance

## 80.12 Stormwater Management

### A. Stormwater Systems
All subdivisions shall provide adequate stormwater management through:
1. Preservation of natural drainage patterns where feasible
2. Construction of drainage improvements as needed
3. Stormwater detention/retention facilities where required
4. Erosion and sediment control measures

### B. Design Standards
Stormwater systems shall:
1. Meet county engineering standards
2. Provide adequate capacity for design storm events
3. Minimize impacts on downstream properties
4. Include appropriate maintenance provisions

## 80.13 Dams and Water Impoundment Structures

Any proposed dams or water impoundment structures shall:
1. Meet all applicable state and federal requirements
2. Be designed by qualified professionals
3. Include appropriate safety measures
4. Receive necessary permits before construction

## 80.14 Street Signs and Traffic Markers

Street signs and traffic control devices shall:
1. Meet county standards for design and placement
2. Provide clear identification of streets and addresses
3. Include appropriate traffic control as determined by county officials
4. Be maintained by the county or appropriate entity

## 80.15 Easements

Required easements shall be provided for:
1. Public utilities and services
2. Drainage and stormwater management
3. Access to public facilities
4. Maintenance of common infrastructure

## 80.16 Monuments

Survey monuments shall be:
1. Installed by licensed land surveyors
2. Placed at all lot corners and property boundaries
3. Constructed to county specifications
4. Protected during construction activities

## 80.18 Subdivision Guarantees

The developer shall provide financial guarantees for:
1. Completion of required improvements
2. Maintenance of improvements during warranty period
3. Correction of defects in workmanship or materials
4. Compliance with approved plans and specifications

Forms of acceptable guarantees include:
- Performance bonds
- Letters of credit
- Escrow deposits
- Other forms approved by the County Commission
