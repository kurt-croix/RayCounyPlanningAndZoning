# Article 70 - Density and Dimensional Standards

## 70.1 Density and Dimensional Standards Table

The following table establishes the density and dimensional standards for each zoning district. These standards represent minimum requirements unless otherwise specified.

### Density Standards
- **R-A Agricultural District**: 1 dwelling unit per 19 acres minimum
- **R-1 Residential Rural District**: 1 dwelling unit per 9 acres minimum  
- **R-1A Residential Low Density District**: 1 dwelling unit per 3 acres minimum
- **R-1B Residential Urban District**: As specified in district regulations
- **R-2 Residential Duplex District**: As specified in district regulations
- **R-3 Residential Multi-Unit District**: Maximum 10 dwelling units per acre
- **R-MHP Residential Mobile Home Park District**: Maximum 7 mobile homes per gross acre

### Dimensional Standards
Specific dimensional standards including setbacks, building height limits, lot coverage, and other dimensional requirements are established for each zoning district as specified in [Article 40](#article-40-base-zoning-districts).

## 70.2 Measurements, Computations and Exceptions

### A. General Measurement Rules
All measurements shall be made using standard surveying practices and shall be measured horizontally unless otherwise specified.

### B. Setback Measurements
Setbacks shall be measured from the nearest point of a structure to the property line, except where specific exceptions are provided in these regulations.

### C. Building Height Measurements
Building height shall be measured from the average finished grade at the foundation to the highest point of the roof structure, excluding chimneys, antennas, and other permitted projections.

### D. Lot Area Computations
Lot area shall include all land within the property boundaries, but shall not include any portion of a public right-of-way.

### E. Exceptions and Projections
Certain architectural features, utilities, and accessory structures may be permitted to project into required setbacks or exceed height limits as specified in these regulations.

### F. Nonconforming Situations
Existing structures that do not meet current dimensional standards may continue as nonconforming structures in accordance with [Article 130](#article-130-nonconformities).
