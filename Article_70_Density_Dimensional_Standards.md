## 70.2 Measurements, Computations and Exceptions

### A. Density

Density refers to the number of [dwelling units](./Article_150_Definitions.md#dwelling-unit) per unit of land area. Density is calculated by dividing the number of [dwelling units](./Article_150_Definitions.md#dwelling-unit) on a site by the gross area (in acres) of the site on which the [dwelling units](./Article_150_Definitions.md#dwelling-unit) are located. The number of [dwelling units](./Article_150_Definitions.md#dwelling-unit) allowed on a site is based on the presumption that all other applicable standards will be met. The maximum density established for a district is not a guarantee that such densities may be obtained, nor a justification for adjusting other dimensional or development standards.

### B. Lot Area

[Lot area](./Article_150_Definitions.md#lot-area) refers to the horizontal land area within [lot lines](./Article_150_Definitions.md#lot-line). No [building](./Article_150_Definitions.md#building) permit or development approval may be issued for a [lot](./Article_150_Definitions.md#lot) that does not meet the minimum [lot area](./Article_150_Definitions.md#lot-area) requirements of these Zoning Regulations except in the following cases:

1. The minimum [lot area](./Article_150_Definitions.md#lot-area) standards of these Zoning Regulations shall not be construed to prohibit the construction of a detached house on a legal [lot](./Article_150_Definitions.md#lot), provided that it is constructed in compliance with all applicable [setback](./Article_150_Definitions.md#setback) standards.

2. Utilities using land or an unoccupied [building](./Article_150_Definitions.md#building) covering less than 1,000 square feet of site area are exempt from the minimum [lot area](./Article_150_Definitions.md#lot-area) standards.

Lot depth is measured from the center (mid-point) of the [front](./Article_150_Definitions.md#yard-front) [lot line](./Article_150_Definitions.md#lot-line) to the center (mid-point) of the [rear](./Article_150_Definitions.md#yard-rear) [lot line](./Article_150_Definitions.md#lot-line).

### E. Setbacks

[Setbacks](./Article_150_Definitions.md#setback) refer to the unobstructed, unoccupied open area between the furthermost projection of a [structure](./Article_150_Definitions.md#structure) and the property line of the [lot](./Article_150_Definitions.md#lot) on which the [structure](./Article_150_Definitions.md#structure) is located, except as modified by the standards of this Article. [Setbacks](./Article_150_Definitions.md#setback) must be unobstructed from the ground to the sky except as specified in this Article.

#### 1. Exceptions to Setbacks

Every part of a required [setback](./Article_150_Definitions.md#setback) must be open and unobstructed from the ground to the sky except as set out in this sub-Article:

a. Trees, shrubbery or other landscape features may be located within any required [setback](./Article_150_Definitions.md#setback);

b. Fences and walls may be located within any required [setback](./Article_150_Definitions.md#setback), subject to [Article 60.3F](./Article_60_Use_Regulations.md#603-accessory-and-temporary-uses);

c. Driveways and sidewalks may be located within any required [setback](./Article_150_Definitions.md#setback);

d. Utility lines, wires and associated [structures](./Article_150_Definitions.md#structure), such as power poles, may be located within any required [setback](./Article_150_Definitions.md#setback).

e. [Signs](./Article_150_Definitions.md#sign), where permitted, may be located within any required [setback](./Article_150_Definitions.md#setback) but may no obstruct views of vehicular traffic;

f. Uncovered porches, uncovered steps to [building](./Article_150_Definitions.md#building) entrances, uncovered patio decks and uncovered balconies may extend up to 10 feet into any required [front](./Article_150_Definitions.md#yard-front), [rear](./Article_150_Definitions.md#yard-rear) or [street](./Article_150_Definitions.md#street) [side](./Article_150_Definitions.md#yard-side) [setback](./Article_150_Definitions.md#setback) and up to 3 feet into any required interior [side](./Article_150_Definitions.md#yard-side) [setback](./Article_150_Definitions.md#setback);

g. Openwork fire balconies and fire escapes may extend up to 3Â½ feet into any required [setback](./Article_150_Definitions.md#setback);

h. Sills, belt courses, cornices, buttresses, eaves and other architectural features may extend up to 3 feet into any required [setback](./Article_150_Definitions.md#setback);

i. Off-[street](./Article_150_Definitions.md#street) parking and loading areas may be located within required [front](./Article_150_Definitions.md#yard-front) or [rear](./Article_150_Definitions.md#yard-rear) [setbacks](./Article_150_Definitions.md#setback), subject to [Article 100.1D.1](./Article_100_Development_Standards.md#1001-parking-requirements);

j. Chimneys and flues may extend up to 2 feet into any required [setback](./Article_150_Definitions.md#setback);

k. [Accessory](./Article_150_Definitions.md#accessory-building-or-use) [structures](./Article_150_Definitions.md#structure) may be located within [setbacks](./Article_150_Definitions.md#setback) only if expressly stated in [Article 60.3](./Article_60_Use_Regulations.md#603-accessory-and-temporary-uses);

l. Solar panels and other apparatus needed for the operation of active and passive solar energy systems, including but not limited to, overhangs, movable insulating walls and roofs, detached solar collectors, reflectors, and piping may project into a required [rear](./Article_150_Definitions.md#yard-rear) [setback](./Article_150_Definitions.md#setback) by up to 10 feet and into a [side](./Article_150_Definitions.md#yard-side) [setback](./Article_150_Definitions.md#setback) by up to 3 feet. In all cases, however, they must be located at least 3 feet from all [lot lines](./Article_150_Definitions.md#lot-line); and

m. Satellite dish antennas may be placed in required [rear](./Article_150_Definitions.md#yard-rear) [setbacks](./Article_150_Definitions.md#setback).

#### 2. Reduction for Public Purpose

When an existing [setback](./Article_150_Definitions.md#setback) is reduced because of conveyance to a federal, state or local government for a public purpose and the remaining [setback](./Article_150_Definitions.md#setback) is at least 50 percent of the required minimum [setback](./Article_150_Definitions.md#setback) for the district in which it is located, then that remaining [setback](./Article_150_Definitions.md#setback) will be deemed to satisfy the minimum [setback](./Article_150_Definitions.md#setback) standards of these Zoning Regulations.

#### 3. Alleys and Rear Setbacks

In computing the depth of a [rear](./Article_150_Definitions.md#yard-rear) [setback](./Article_150_Definitions.md#setback) for any [lot](./Article_150_Definitions.md#lot) abutting an [alley](./Article_150_Definitions.md#alley), one-half the width of such [alley](./Article_150_Definitions.md#alley) may be included in the [rear](./Article_150_Definitions.md#yard-rear) [setback](./Article_150_Definitions.md#setback) dimension.

### F. Building Coverage

[Building](./Article_150_Definitions.md#building) coverage refers to the area of a [lot](./Article_150_Definitions.md#lot) covered by [buildings](./Article_150_Definitions.md#building) ([principal](./Article_150_Definitions.md#building-principal) and [accessory](./Article_150_Definitions.md#accessory-building-or-use)) or roofed areas, as measured along the outside wall at ground level, and including all projections, other than open porches, fire escapes, canopies and the first 2 feet of a roof overhang.

### G. Height

[Building height](./Article_150_Definitions.md#building-height) refers to the vertical distance between the average finished grade (based on an average of the highest and lowest ground points) at the base of the [building](./Article_150_Definitions.md#building) being measured and: 1) the average height level between the eaves and ridge line of a gable, hip or gambrel roof; 2) the highest point of a mansard roof; or 3) the highest point of the coping of a flat roof.

#### 1. Fences or Walls

In the case of fences or walls, height shall be measured from ground level on the higher side of the fence or wall.

#### 2. Exemptions from Height Limits

The height limitations contained herein shall not apply to any of the following:

a. grain bins or other farm buildings in the R-A and R-1 districts;

b. electrical power transmission lines;

c. belfries, cupolas, spires, domes, monuments, flagpoles, chimneys, radio/television receiving antennas or chimney flues; or

d. bulkhead, elevator, water tank, or any other similar structure or necessary mechanical appurtenance extending above the roof of any building if such structure does not occupy more than 33 percent of the area of the roof.

#### 3. Exceptions for Towers and Antennae

Towers and antennae for 2-way or commercial broadcast, wireless telecommunications, or towers for wind-powered generators or pumps may be allowed to extend above the district height standards if approved by [Conditional Use](./Article_150_Definitions.md#conditional-use) Permit.
