# Article 120 - Signs

## 120.1 General

This Article establishes regulations for signs to:
1. Promote the public health, safety, and welfare
2. Enhance the visual environment of the county
3. Allow adequate identification and advertising while minimizing visual clutter
4. Protect property values and community character

## 120.2 Definitions

**Animated Sign** - A sign that uses movement or change of lighting to depict action or create a special effect or scene.

**Banner** - A sign made of fabric, plastic, paper, or similar non-rigid material that is designed to be temporarily displayed.

**Billboard** - A large outdoor sign, typically found along highways and major roads, used for advertising.

**Business Sign** - A sign that directs attention to a business or profession conducted upon the premises where the sign is located.

**Directory Sign** - A sign listing the names and locations of occupants or activities within a building or development.

**Electronic Message Sign** - A sign that displays information through the use of LED lights, liquid crystal displays, or similar technology.

**Freestanding Sign** - A sign supported by one or more posts, poles, or braces placed in or upon the ground.

**Identification Sign** - A sign giving the name, address, or other identification of a building, business, development, or establishment.

**Illuminated Sign** - A sign designed to give forth artificial light through transparent or translucent material or from an artificial light source directed upon the sign surface.

**Monument Sign** - A low-profile freestanding sign where the base of the sign is on or close to the ground.

**Pole Sign** - A freestanding sign mounted on one or more poles or supports.

**Portable Sign** - A sign that is designed to be moved from place to place and is not permanently attached to a structure or the ground.

**Projecting Sign** - A sign attached to and projecting from a building wall.

**Roof Sign** - A sign erected upon or above a roof or parapet wall of a building.

**Temporary Sign** - A sign intended for display for a limited time period.

**Wall Sign** - A sign attached to or painted upon the exterior wall of a building.

**Window Sign** - A sign placed on or visible through a window.

## 120.3 Computations and Measurements

### A. Sign Area
Sign area shall be calculated as the area within the smallest rectangle that encompasses all elements of the sign, including frame and background.

### B. Sign Height
Sign height shall be measured from the average grade at the base of the sign to the highest point of the sign structure.

### C. Setback Measurements
Sign setbacks shall be measured from the nearest point of the sign structure to the property line.

## 120.4 Signs Exempt from Regulation

The following signs are exempt from the permit requirements but must comply with all other applicable regulations:
1. Government signs
2. Traffic control signs
3. Warning and safety signs
4. Address identification signs not exceeding 2 square feet
5. Real estate signs not exceeding 32 square feet
6. Political campaign signs (temporary)

## 120.5 Prohibited Sign Types

The following signs are prohibited in all zoning districts:
1. Animated or flashing signs (except electronic message signs where permitted)
2. Signs that obstruct vision at intersections
3. Signs that imitate traffic control devices
4. Off-premise advertising signs (billboards) except where specifically permitted
5. Roof-mounted signs
6. Signs in public rights-of-way (except government signs)

## 120.6 Location

### A. Setback Requirements
Signs shall maintain appropriate setbacks from property lines as specified for each zoning district.

### B. Vision Clearance
Signs shall not obstruct vision clearance areas at intersections.

### C. Utility Clearance
Signs shall maintain adequate clearance from overhead utility lines.

## 120.7 Permitted Sign Types

### A. Wall Signs
Wall signs are permitted in all nonresidential zoning districts and shall not exceed specified percentages of the wall area to which they are attached.

### B. Freestanding Signs
Freestanding signs are permitted in nonresidential zoning districts, subject to height and area limitations.

### C. Directory Signs
Directory signs are permitted for multi-tenant buildings and developments.

### D. Identification Signs
Small identification signs are permitted in residential districts for home occupations and similar uses.

## 120.8 Special Sign Standards

### A. Electronic Message Signs
Electronic message signs may be permitted in commercial and industrial districts, subject to:
1. Limitations on message change frequency
2. Brightness controls
3. Automatic dimming for nighttime operation

### B. Temporary Signs
Temporary signs may be permitted for special events, construction projects, and similar short-term activities.

### C. Political Signs
Political campaign signs are permitted during designated time periods before and after elections.

## 120.9 Maximum Total Sign Area Per Lot

Total sign area per lot shall not exceed:
- **Residential districts**: 32 square feet
- **Commercial districts**: 200 square feet or 2 square feet per linear foot of street frontage, whichever is greater
- **Industrial districts**: 300 square feet

## 120.10 Number and Dimensions

### A. Number of Signs
The number of freestanding signs per lot shall be limited based on the zoning district and lot size.

### B. Maximum Dimensions
Maximum height and area for signs shall be specified for each sign type and zoning district.

## 120.11 Building Signs in Nonresidential Districts

Building-mounted signs in nonresidential districts shall:
1. Not exceed specified percentages of wall area
2. Maintain appropriate clearances from windows and doors
3. Be professionally designed and installed

## 120.12 Sign Characteristics

### A. Materials
Signs shall be constructed of durable, weather-resistant materials appropriate for their intended use and location.

### B. Colors
Sign colors should be compatible with the building and surrounding area.

### C. Lighting
Sign lighting shall be designed to minimize glare and light pollution.

## 120.13 Design, Construction, and Maintenance

### A. Design Standards
Signs shall be professionally designed to enhance the visual environment and be compatible with surrounding development.

### B. Construction Standards
All signs shall be constructed in accordance with applicable building and electrical codes.

### C. Maintenance Requirements
Sign owners shall maintain their signs in good condition and remove or repair damaged or deteriorated signs.

### D. Permits and Inspections
Sign permits shall be obtained before installation, and signs shall be subject to inspection for code compliance.
